Two months ago — February 15 — I started building Glitch Executor.

Since then: 17+ production repositories, three live product lines, a voice AI running in production, a 9-model trading ensemble, and sports intelligence engines for cricket and NBA.

That sounds like a list designed to impress. It's actually the consequence of one realization I had early on, and kept testing.

The realization: no single AI model wins across all conditions. Coordinated ensembles do.

I found it in trading first. A momentum strategy prints in trending markets and gets destroyed in ranging ones. Mean reversion does the opposite. I stopped trying to pick the right model and started coordinating many of them under an Oracle layer that sees the whole portfolio.

Then I noticed the same shape of problem in sports pricing.

Then in ad attribution.

Then in e-commerce ops.

Every time I tested it, the pattern held. So I kept applying it.

That's why three product lines exist. They're not unrelated bets. They're the same bet tested in three different markets.

The honest version of where we are at two months in: some of this is live and performing. Some is active and I'm still figuring out the edge. None of it is "results guaranteed."

The part I find most interesting: the coordination layer is always the hard part. The individual models are commodities now. The Oracle — the thing that sees the whole picture — is where the actual work lives.

Starting to post here about decisions, lessons, and what building this at this pace actually looks like. Not the highlight reel.
