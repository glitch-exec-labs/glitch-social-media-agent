Most AI companies launch with a demo.

We're introducing ourselves with 17+ production repositories, three live product lines, and one thesis we've tested across all of them.

We're Glitch Executor — an AI lab based in Toronto.

The thesis: no single AI model wins across all conditions. Coordinated ensembles do.

We found it in trading. A momentum model prints in trending markets and gets destroyed in ranging ones. Mean reversion does the opposite. We stopped trying to pick the right model and started coordinating many of them under an Oracle layer that sees the full portfolio. That's our Ouroboros ensemble — Viper, Cobra, Mamba, Anaconda, Hydra, and four more, each with a distinct strategy personality.

Then we noticed the same shape of problem in sports pricing. Then voice AI. Then ad attribution.

So we applied the same pattern everywhere it fit.

Today we run three domains:

Trade — the ensemble above, broker-portable across MT5 and cTrader.

Edge — cricket and NBA intelligence engines, plus a bilingual Hindi/English voice agent that confirms COD orders for Indian e-commerce customers before dispatch. Live in production.

Grow — a Meta-to-Amazon attribution bridge recovering the 60% of Meta-driven purchases that land on Amazon and are invisible to the Shopify pixel.

Everything we build is public. github.com/glitch-exec-labs
